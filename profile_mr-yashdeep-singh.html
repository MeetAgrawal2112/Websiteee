Biography & Research

 

Mr. Yashdeep Singh is currently working as an Adjunct Assistant Professor in the Department of Computer Science and Engineering at the Indian Institute of Information Technology (IIIT) Pune, India. He completed his M.Tech. in Computer Science and Engineering from the Indian Institute of Technology Guwahati and is currently completing his Ph.D. at IIT Guwahati, where his thesis has been submitted and the defense is awaited.

His research focuses on preference-based matching, popular matching, approximation algorithms, and structural graph theory. He has published in international journals and conferences, including Concurrency and Computation: Practice and Experience (CCPE), IWOCA, and CANDAR. His work addresses hardness results and approximation guarantees for generalized matching models.

Yashdeep has two years of experience as an Assistant Professor at Jaypee University of Information Technology, where he taught undergraduate courses such as Algorithms, Theory of Computation, Database Management Systems, and Algorithm Design. He also served as a Teaching Assistant at IIT Guwahati during his M.Tech. and Ph.D. His interests include algorithm design, combinatorial optimization, and fair allocation mechanisms.
